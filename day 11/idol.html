My Idols

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj was the founder of the Maratha Empire in India. Born in 1630 at Shivneri Fort, he was a brilliant military strategist and administrator. He established a competent and progressive civil rule and is revered for his courage, leadership, and respect for women. His legacy lives on as a symbol of valor and good governance.

Aryabhata

Aryabhata was a renowned Indian mathematician and astronomer of the classical age. Born in 476 CE, he authored the famous work "Aryabhatiya", which introduced the concept of zero and explained the heliocentric model centuries before Europe. His contributions greatly influenced Indian and global science.

Lord Krishna

Lord Krishna, a central figure in the Mahabharata and the speaker of the Bhagavad Gita, is considered the 8th incarnation of Lord Vishnu. Known for his wisdom, charm, and divine leadership, he played a crucial role in guiding Arjuna and promoting the path of dharma. His teachings continue to inspire millions globally.

Mahendra Singh Dhoni

MS Dhoni is a legendary Indian cricketer and former captain of the Indian national team. Under his leadership, India won the 2007 T20 World Cup, 2011 ODI World Cup, and the 2013 Champions Trophy. Known for his calm demeanor and sharp strategies, Dhoni remains an inspiration for youth and sports lovers.

Dr. Homi Bhabha

Dr. Homi Jehangir Bhabha was an Indian nuclear physicist and the chief architect of India's nuclear program. He established the Tata Institute of Fundamental Research (TIFR) and was instrumental in laying the foundation of India’s atomic energy development. His vision continues to guide India’s scientific progress.
